# AI Budget Challenge Format

Use when Sharbel develops a viral challenge video comparing cheap vs expensive AI setups.

## Proven packaging

Default to the broad viral title formula, not a technical benchmark title:

- Primary title: `$0 vs $10,000 AI Challenge`
- Thumbnail text: `$0 vs $10K`
- Avoid over-explaining the economics in the title.
- Avoid `AI model` when the video is really a build challenge, unless Sharbel explicitly wants the benchmark framing.

The title sells the contrast. The intro explains the mechanics.

## Strong tier ladder

For this format, prefer four tiers:

1. `$0`: free AI only
2. `$20`: first paid AI subscription/coding assistant tier
3. `$100`: premium coding setup, for example Claude Code/Max-style serious solo-builder tier
4. Either `$1,000` or `$10,000`, but not both

Sharbel preferred keeping the final packaging as `$0 vs $10,000 AI Challenge` because it is the viral proven title formula.

## $10,000 tier realism

Do not claim there is a normal consumer `$10,000/month AI subscription`.

Frame it as a `$10,000/month AI operator stack` or `$10,000/month AI agent setup`.

For a one-hour challenge, prorate the monthly stack:

- `$10,000/month`
- `20 working days/month`
- `8 working hours/day`
- `$500/day`
- `$62.50/hour`

So the high tier can say: it has access to a `$10,000/month AI stack`, but for this one-hour sprint it only gets the hourly equivalent budget of `$62.50`.

This preserves the viral `$10,000` contrast while staying defensible.

## Core angle

The deeper question is not just which model is smarter.

Use this framing:

> Does expensive AI actually build better products, or does it just make the same bad ideas look more polished?

And later:

> Expensive AI does not just buy a smarter model. It buys a better operating system around the model.

The final comparison should test leverage under pressure, not just intelligence.

## Challenge mission

A strong mission for this format:

> Build a tiny AI product for creators that could realistically make its first dollar.

Each tier should create:

1. A specific product idea
2. A working demo or prototype
3. A landing page
4. A clear pricing strategy
5. Optionally, a first-customer plan

Keep all tiers building for the same customer type so the comparison stays fair.

## Judging criteria

Judge by:

- Product clarity: can a creator understand it instantly?
- Usefulness: does it solve a real creator pain?
- Demo quality: can it actually be clicked, run, or shown?
- Speed to money: is there a believable first dollar?
- Honesty: did it fake traction, overpromise, or build demo-only slop?

## Tier positioning

`$0`: constraint creates clarity. It should avoid complexity and build a tiny useful artifact.

`$20`: first paid AI builder. Expect better polish and a product-shaped prototype, but watch for fake SaaS theater.

`$100`: serious solo founder. Capability is no longer the excuse, so judge product decisions, narrow customer, and sellability.

`$10,000`: AI operator stack. The test is coordination, not raw intelligence: specialist agents, premium models, paid APIs, data, automation, and the prorated hourly spend.

## Style notes

- Keep the visible title simple: `$0 vs $10,000 AI Challenge`.
- Put the tier nuance in the intro, not the packaging.
- Use clean spoken prose from the hook onward.
- No em dashes.
- Do not show the script framework or strategy brief in the Notion script unless Sharbel explicitly asks.
