# Viral TikTok AI Hacks YouTube Format

Use this when the creator wants a YouTube video built around viral TikTok AI hacks or tools.

## Core lesson
Do not build this as a generic "best AI tools" list. The viral format is: TikTok makes a specific promise, then the creator tests whether it survives real operator work.

Each segment needs an actual individual TikTok video receipt URL. Do not use broad list TikToks, TikTok search links, or placeholder receipt notes as main segment evidence unless explicitly framed as intro proof only.

## Segment template
For every hack/tool:
1. TikTok receipt: exact TikTok video URL, creator, views, likes, shares when available.
2. TikTok promise: one sentence describing the claim.
3. the creator's skepticism: what might be fake, cherry-picked, or only demo-friendly.
4. Real test: a concrete operator task from the creator's workflow.
5. Result: show the artifact/output/failure on screen.
6. Verdict: Fake, Mid, Real, or Keeper.

## Framing
Best title direction: "I Tested the Most Viral TikTok AI Hacks"

Thumbnail text options:
- REAL OR FAKE?
- TIKTOK LIED?
- 3 SURVIVED

Intro promise:
"I pulled viral AI hacks from TikTok, then tested each one with real work. If it only works in the TikTok demo, it dies. If it saves real time or creates something usable, it moves on."

## Script requirements
Always apply the `scriptwriting` skill / YouTube script PDF framework before drafting. Keep the framework invisible in the delivered script. From the hook onward, deliver clean spoken prose, not labeled framework sections.

## Pitfalls
- Do not claim these are mathematically the top 10 TikToks on the platform unless you have actually verified that with a reliable ranked source.
- Phrase safely: "the most viral AI hacks I could verify and actually test on camera."
- Do not include broad AI-tool list videos as main test segments. They can be used in the intro montage as category proof.
- Do not invent TikTok links or metrics. If a link cannot be verified, label it as a search lead, not a receipt.
- Avoid making the video too developer-heavy. Mix builder, voice, learning, video, app, image, and agent hacks for broad appeal.
